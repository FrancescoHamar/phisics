Kinematics is the study of how do objects move. Lets answer this question together.



Kinematics in two dimensions. Click & drag to spawn.

Realize how when you drag and slingshot the ball, you are giving it horizontal and vertical compononent vectors to its velocity. The components will behave independent of each other until the ball hits the ground, meaning they share time and nothing else! To derive the component vectors, simply imagine a triangle and use trigonometry. Hint: h*cosθ and h*sinθ.

Vertical motion

Constant acceleration due to gravity:

Vf = Vi + at

Δd = Vit + (1/2)at²

Δd = (Vi+Vf)/2

Vf² = Vi² + 2aΔd

Horizontal motion

Constant velocity remains unchanged:

V = Δd/t
