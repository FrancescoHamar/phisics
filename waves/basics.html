Graphing waves

Waves are sinusoidal functions, meaning they can be either expressed with cosine or sine graphs. The amplitude of a wave is its maximum displacement (notation: x0). The period is the time it takes for the wave to repeat.

x = x0cos(ωt)

ω = 2πf

ω² = k/m

T = 1/f

f = 1/T



Further understanding

Several realizations appear from this phenomenon. Firstly, acceleration is always proportional to negative displacement. Given that, an oscillating object has maximum acceleration at the crest and trough, while velocity is maximum at the point of equilibrium (x = 0). Here are some formulas that describe this behaviour.

a = -ω²x

amax = -ω²x0

vmax = x0ω

v(t) = -x0ωsin(ωt)

a(t) = x0cos(ωt)

KE(t) = (1/2)mx0²ω²sin(ωt)

PEel(t) = (1/2)kx0²cos²(ωt)
